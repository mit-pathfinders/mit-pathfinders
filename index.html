December 6th to January 24th
Preliminary Round Starts Nov 1 on the Slack

Learn More Register


MIT Pathfinders is an opportunity for high school and college students to participate in real world digital pandemic response projects. Be it through code or writing, we can’t wait to see how your ideas help get the world back on track.

What

An competition to support the digital pandemic response with tasks ranging from research, writing, and development! Learn more about the format here.

Who

Students aged 13+

Where

100% Virtual on the MIT Pathfinders Slack

When

December 6th to January 24th

Tracks

You can compete in one or both tracks

Open-Source Track

The MIT PathFinders open-source competition is open to 13+ year old students. Guided by professional mentors, competitors will work on open-source tasks for research and development teams working on digital pandemic response.

Tasks can be from various categories, including but not limited to, app development, data science, epidemiology, simulations, and documentation.

This track is split by age into a 13-15 and 15-18 division.

Rules & Format
Writing Track

The MIT Pathfinders essay competition is open to 13-18 year old students. Through this contest, students will explore the uses of cutting-edge technology in the world's digital pandemic response.

All students who submit an essay will be invited to join PathCheck Foundation’s global network of volunteers, so that they can get involved firsthand in the technologies they are writing about.

This track is split by age into a 13-18 and 18+ division.

F.A.Q

  • Do I need to know how to code?

    No! The essay track doesn't require programming experience, and there are also several tasks in the open-source track that do not require previous coding experience. However, for the open source track, a background in Javascript (React, Node) or Python is useful but not necessary.

  • How do the organizers pick the tasks?

    The tasks were chosen to serve the real time needs of the organization. Thus, your efforts during the competition will directly contribute to the success of PathCheck’s mission to reopen society.

  • I will be turning 13 after the beginning of the contest, can I still participate?

    Unfortunately, we are only allowing participants who are within the eligible age range on the day of the competition.

  • How would I become a finalist?

    Finalists are chosen according to the number and difficulty of the tasks they complete. The top five performers overall will be named finalists.

  • What if I need help with my task?

    Don't worry! We'll have college and professional mentors to guide you through your tasks.

  • I'm in college, can I participate?

    If your age is 13-18, you can participate! If you are older than 18, you can volunteer as a mentor to help students with their tasks. Shoot us an email at pathfinders@pathcheck.org if you're interested!


Have any other questions? Email us at pathfinders@pathcheck.org!
