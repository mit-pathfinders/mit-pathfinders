MIT Pathfinders is a joint event with the MIT Vaccine Conference for students to write, present, and discuss essays about vaccination in the coronavirus pandemic.

What

A virtual meetup where students can learn and write about vaccination and vital information regarding the pandemic!

Who

High school students aged 13+

Where

100% Virtual!

When

December 11th

Essay Information

Learn more about the essay

Prompt

Write an essay discussing vaccination as it relates to the COVID-19 pandemic. Consider vaccine development and/or distribution. This can include implications, problems, comparisons, etc. of various vaccines. You may include examples of vaccines that are currently being developed as well as look ahead to future pandemics. Also, consider placing vaccination in the broader scopes of fighting the pandemic or advancements in technology. Use your imagination backed with research and think outside the box! Please contact us if there are any questions about the prompt!

Guidelines

Please submit your essay following these guidelines:

  • 1500 words max: excluding abstract and referances
  • Abstract/Claim (300-500 words)
  • Intro/Background
  • Methods
  • Discussions
  • References (MLA format)

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • I will be turning 13 after the beginning of the event, can I still participate?

      Unfortunately, we are only allowing participants who are within the eligible age range on the day of the event.

    • Do I get a certificate?

      All authors whose essays are published on the MIT Pathfinders website will receive a digitally signed certificate from MIT Media Lab Professor Ramesh Raskar.

    • Do I need to be a student to attend?

      The Pathfinders event taking place on December 11th is open to everyone. Only participants submitting the essay must be high schoolers in the United States and aged 13+.

    • What if I need help with my task?

      Don't worry! We'll have professional mentors to guide you through your tasks.

    • I'm in college, can I participate?

      College students will be eligible to attend the presentation on December 11th, but unfortunately will not be eligible to submit an essay.
